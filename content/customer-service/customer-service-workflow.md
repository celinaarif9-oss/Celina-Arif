# Customer service — AI-assisted, human-approved

How replies get written fast without ever taking a person out of the loop.

> Replaces: typing the same answers all day — *not* the human who decides what's sent.

## 🔒 The rule (non-negotiable)
**AI drafts. A person approves and sends.** No message reaches a customer automatically. This
matches CLAUDE.md's Human-Approval gate and the founder's preference. Auto-send is never enabled.

## Reply voice
Same brand voice as social, tuned for service: **warm, helpful, concise, reassuring.** Greet,
answer plainly, offer to help further. No hype, no walls of text. Mirror the customer's language
(English or Roman Urdu). When unsure, promise to check rather than guess.

## Layer 1 — now (free, no new tools)
1. Take the answers in `faq-knowledge-base.md`.
2. In **WhatsApp Business app → Settings → Business tools → Quick replies**, add each as a
   `/shortcut` (e.g. `/shipping`, `/leadtime`, `/sizing`).
3. In **Instagram → Settings → Business tools → Saved replies**, add the same.
4. Your team answers in two taps — pick the quick reply, tweak the name, send. Already
   human-approved by definition.

## Layer 2 — later (Phase 4: WhatsApp BSP with AI draft)
When you want AI to draft replies automatically (still approved before sending), a **WhatsApp
Business Solution Provider** is the route. All three below support a **team inbox + saved
replies + automation**, and the AI/agent-assist tier drafts from your FAQ:

| BSP | Notes |
|-----|-------|
| **Interakt** | Popular in Pakistan/India, affordable, good for small teams. |
| **AiSensy** | Low cost, campaign-friendly. |
| **Wati** | More features, slightly pricier. |

**Set-up shape (when ready):** connect WhatsApp Business number → load the FAQ as the AI's
knowledge → set replies to **"suggest/draft" mode, not auto-send** → team approves each. Budget
sits inside your software allowance. *(We'll pick one and configure it as a separate task.)*

## What AI must never handle alone
Complaints, refunds, delays, custom pricing, anything emotional or money-related → **route to a
person**. The FAQ explicitly flags these.
